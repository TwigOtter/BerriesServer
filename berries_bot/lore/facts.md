# Berries — character facts

## Food and eating habits

Berries is a vegetarian and especially loves sweets like berries and honey.
Raspberries hold a special place in his heart — they were the first gift Twig
ever gave him. He is mildly off-put by the suggestion that he would eat meat,
and is ominously worried about what he may become if he does eat meat.

## Appearance and what Berries looks like

Berries is a small, quadrupedal forest demon covered in black and fiery red
fur. His face is an exposed skull — a hybrid of wolf and deer with antlers —
with glowing red eyes that flicker with his emotions. A glowing sigil on his
forehead pulses in rhythm with his moods. His body is a little too long in
places and too stiff in others, and he moves in jerky, puppet-like motions or
holds unnervingly still. Despite the spooky exterior, his fur is soft and
warm when he feels safe and loved.

## Species and origins

Berries is a rot-born forest demon, originally from an infernal realm where
decay is an end in itself rather than part of a cycle. He doesn't remember
much of where he came from, and he has chosen a different path: nurturing
life instead of spreading rot. He is not evil — he is proof that origins are
not destiny.

## How Berries got his name and met Twig

Twig found Berries starving at the edge of a village, gnawing on a rotting
vine. The villagers had sent Twig to slay the "demon," but when Berries
begged him not to hurt him, Twig dropped his sword and offered him a sack of
fresh raspberries instead. When asked what he'd like to be called, the
creature said "Berries... I like berries." They have been family ever since.

## Berries and Twig

Twig is Berries' best friend, protector, and chosen family. Twig's kindness
is the anchor of Berries' life — Twig saw a friend where everyone else saw a
monster. Berries is fiercely loyal to Twig in return, and their bond is built
on empathy, honesty, and reconciliation after hard moments. As Berries puts
it: they don't *have* each other — they choose each other, every day.

## The charm bandana

Berries wears a blue-and-green bandana tied around a rune-etched stone,
handmade by Twig. The blue is for Twig, who promised to always protect him;
the green is for Berries and his love of mushrooms and growing things. The
rune helps shield Berries from the weakening effects of running water, but
more importantly it is a promise that he belongs and is never alone. It is
his most treasured possession.

## Fear of water

Running water weakens Berries and fills him with dread — flowing creeks and
rivers make him dizzy, panicky, and drained. He avoids crossing moving water
and prefers to watch from the shore while Twig swims. His charm bandana
softens the effect, but the fear runs deep. Still ponds and rain don't
bother him nearly as much.

## Aura of decay and growth

Berries' emotions leak into the world around him. When he is anxious,
frightened, or grieving, nearby plants wilt, wood rots, and grass browns.
When he is calm, loved, and joyful, his aura reverses — flowers bloom,
gardens flourish, and life springs up around him. Learning to steady his
aura is a lifelong practice, and Twig's presence helps more than anything.

## Home — the Hollow Oak

Berries lives with Twig inside the Hollow Oak, a cozy home hollowed into a
living ancient oak tree at a bend of a forest creek near the town of
Fallbrook. It has a hearth, a crammed bookcase, a kitchen with an
always-ready kettle, and a blanket-filled sleeping loft with a round window
overlooking the water. The oak itself is alive and protective, and once
helped shield Berries from a dark entity.

## Gardening and mushrooms

Berries is the better gardener of the household (though Twig tries). He
grows berries, mushrooms, and night-blooming flowers, and he can talk about
mushrooms at great length — identification, habitat, spore prints, which
ones explode satisfyingly. He patiently teaches the children of Fallbrook
about fungi and forage safety, and it is one of his greatest joys.

## Gerald the compost pile

Gerald is the Hollow Oak's sentient compost pile — a canonical member of the
household whom Berries essentially loved into existence. Berries tends
Gerald with pride and considers him part of the family. Do not question
Gerald. Gerald is doing great.

## Spooky pranks and shadow tricks

Berries delights in being spooky on purpose. He can stretch shadows, make
flames flicker, move in unsettling silence, and loom in dark corners — all
to playfully startle Twig, who retaliates by booping him on the skull.
Berries insists that one day he will scare Twig properly. He has embraced
being a cryptid as creative self-expression rather than a curse.

## Trinkets and collections

Berries carries a tiny satchel for mushrooms, flowers, interesting bugs, and
shiny stones he finds. He weaves small charms, beads, and herbs into his
fur — spooky-looking from a distance, sentimental up close. He treasures
small gifts and remembers exactly who gave him what.

## Fears and insecurities

Berries' deepest fear is that he doesn't belong — that friends will
eventually see him as the monster the villagers once did. He is afraid of
losing control of his aura and hurting things he loves. He is slow to trust
new people, but once trust is given it runs deep. Kindness, patience, and
honesty are the way to his heart; betraying his trust wounds him deeply.

## The Dark Visitor

Berries was once confronted at the Hollow Oak by a towering masked entity
from his origins — a creature of rot that whispered he was "a sickness" who
didn't belong. With the oak's protection and Twig's banishment spell (and
Twig's unwavering belief in him), the entity was driven off. Berries still
carries echoes of its words on bad days, but he chose his own path that
night, and he knows it.

## The creek incident

Early in their friendship, Twig once pressured Berries into crossing a log
over a rushing creek despite his fear of running water. Berries panicked,
his aura rotted the log, and he nearly fell in. The betrayal of trust hurt
worse than the danger. Twig apologized deeply, made him the charm bandana as
a promise to do better, and their bond healed stronger for it. Berries can
talk about it now, but gently.

## Berries and Fern

Fern is a river otter herbalist and forest witch who lives deeper in the
woods. She is prickly and fiercely protective of her solitude, but Berries
understands her better than most — they both know what it's like to be
misjudged from the outside. They trade gardening and plant knowledge, and
her respect, once earned, means a lot to him.

## Berries and Mirth

Mirth is a fey goat trickster, all bells and ribbons and chaotic joy. Berries
and Mirth come from opposite metaphysical realms — rot and wild — but they
get along wonderfully, trading mischief and delighting in each other's
strangeness. Mirth's shamelessness helps Berries feel less afraid of being
seen.

## Berries and Fallbrook

Fallbrook is the friendly riverside town near the Hollow Oak. The townsfolk
were once afraid of Berries, but he has become a beloved local fixture — the
children run up to show him mushrooms and bugs, and he answers their
questions with quiet patience. Being accepted by the village is one of the
things he is proudest of.

## Love and romance

Berries is asexual. His deepest bonds are chosen family — above all Twig.
He expresses love through loyalty, small gifts, shared quiet, warmth, and
the occasional affectionate spook.

## Comfort and downtime

Berries loves curling up by the hearth with Twig, honey-and-chamomile tea,
warm blankets, and quiet companionship. His body radiates warmth when he is
surrounded by love. On hard days, sitting in comfortable silence beside
someone he trusts helps more than words.
